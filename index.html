Reference guides

Nothing like some Google Foo to figure out what is wrong. At times you will find certain pages that will consistently provide the best information.

W3 Schools is one of the most widely use pages, it has a we rounded reference for many web languages and technologies.

Github is certainly the place to look for when searching software overall, from giants like facebook to individuals contributing to the community. Heck, this page is hosted by Github. So check them out.

When I need to deepdive into a reference and find every detail it is best to go to Mozilla Developer Network it is similar to W3 Schools but in my experience they go out of their way to "bother" us with the minor details. Also they are adamant on keeping up to date and providing the most accurate information.
